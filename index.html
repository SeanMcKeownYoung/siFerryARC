Rough Seas for
Staten Islanders.

The problem involves many, yet no heroes have emerged to rescue those who depend on the boats. Here's who is involved and the role they play.

The Staten Island Ferry, a critical link to Manhattan, used to be known as one of the most reliable forms of mass transit in New York City. Yet ongoing schedule reductions have upended riders, who can no longer depend on the 24-hour ferry service. So what's to blame? The factors range from strict licensing requirements, to labor disputes, short staffing and, in some people's mind, a lack of urgency by the city.

New York City Department of Transportation
The New York City Department of Transportation, operator of the Staten Island Ferry, has been unable to adequately staff its boats in recent years, prompting recurring service reductions, particularly during afternoon and overnight hours.
Marine Engineers Beneficial Association (MEBA)
The Marine Engineers Beneficial Association, which represents various Staten Island Ferry crew titles, claims that the city's unwillingness to offer competitive wages during a longstanding contract dispute is the reason the boats have been short-staffed and service has been inconsistent..
United States Coast Guard
The United States Coast Guard sets forth the licensing requirements and necessary certifications for Staten Island Ferry crew members. The Coast Guard also mandates that each vessel be staffed with at least one captain, one assistant captain and various other positions in order to legally operate.
Mayor of New York City
The mayor of New York City is responsible for administering all city agencies, including the Department of Transportation, which operates the Staten Island Ferry, the Office of Labor Relations, which is responsible for negotiating union contracts, and the Office of Management and Budget, which determines city funding for various agencies. The ongoing contract dispute has now spanned three mayoral administration, with no resolution in sight.
New York City Office of Labor Relations
The New York City Office of Labor Relations represents Mayor Eric Adams in all negotiations between the City of New York and labor unions. Over the past decade, the Office of Labor Relations has reached contract agreements with dozens of unions, with the notable exception of the Marine Engineers Beneficial Association.
New York City Office of Management and Budget
The New York City Office of Management and Budget develops and executes NYC’s budget, determining the amount of funding that will be issued annually to each city agency. Additional funding would likely need to be provided to the Department of Transportation to accommodate higher wages for Staten Island Ferry workers.
New York City Council
The New York City Council monitors city agencies to ensure they’re effectively serving New Yorkers. Despite the repeated service disruptions on the Staten Island Ferry, which are in violation of a local law requiring half-hourly service, the City Council has struggled to hold the Department of Transportation accountable.
